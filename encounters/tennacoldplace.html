During Tenna's turn, the screen at the back displays "GAME TIME!" instead of the current score, and his attack is replaced by a minigame sequence (or two minigames, from use 6 onwards). This involves Tenna jumping to the center of the encounter and pressing his hands to either side of his head as his screen turns to static. The camera zooms in on his screen, which transitions to a randomly chosen minigame. Instructions are displayed at the center of the screen depending on the game:

  • DODGE FIRE for the Cooking Show
  • PERFORM for Rock Video (with variations 2x SPEED, 2.5x SPEED, and 3x SPEED)
  • PROTECT RALSEI for a version of Monster Movie based around defending Ralsei from hostile Tennas (available only if said game was played to completion at least once; the timer bar is replaced by a health bar labelled ENEMY)
  • FIGHT... for an alternate version of Monster Movie based around dodging and attacking a hostile Tenna (also available only if said game was played to completion at least once)
  • SHOOT THE TARGETS for a version of Shadowguy's {% include acticon chara="Susie" -%} {% include acticon chara="Ralsei" -%} Sharpshoot ACT
  • SHOOT for the cowboy game
  • DODGE for a reversed version of the cowboy game
  • BOUNCE THE BALL for a weather-themed ball-bouncing attack

(Almost all these instructions should end with an exclamation mark ("DODGE FIRE!", "PERFORM!" etc.) while "FIGHT..." should end with a question mark i.e. "FIGHT...?", but the object in charge of the instructions stops writing out the text early in an off-by-one error.)

For most minigames (unless specified otherwise above), a bar on the bottom left acts as a timer, with a counter underneath starting at "SCORE 000" and increasing with time passed/correct gameplay (depending on the game). On the bottom right are three checkboxes labelled "MISS", which are marked off as mistakes are made. At the end of the minigame(s), the POINTs earned are added to the score, and penalties are dealt in both POINTs and HP for any MISSes. If 3 mistakes are made, the minigame sequence ends early. See the Deltarune Wiki for more detail on gameplay for each individual minigame.

End of fight

Plays on the turn immediately after the score reaches 1000 or above, or Tenna's HP reaches 20% or below.

Tenna That's it! It's time for the FINAL EPISODE! Tenna What will happen to our HEROES... Tenna When they REALIZE this show is NEVER gonna end!?

Tenna triggers a minigame sequence that switches rapidly between all minigames used previously, cutting between them abruptly and not giving any instructions or breathing room. In the top right, a green counter starts at Ch00 and increases by 1 with each successive switch between games (even if the game has shown up before as a previous "channel"). The usual minigame UI is also absent, with no timer, score, or miss counter; instead, damage is dealt as soon as mistakes are made.

After several minigames are played in quick succession, the screen returns to the original set and the instruction "DEFEAT TENNA" appears. A bar on the bottom labelled "ENEMY" represents Tenna's "HP". Using the Sharpshoot mechanism, the party must destroy projectiles and shoot Tenna until his health bar is reduced to 0.

Upon taking the final hit, Tenna reels in shock and the screen fades to white. After a moment, it fades back in to the stage, where Tenna now stands clutching his head, his screen black. With a brief burst of static both over the full game screen and Tenna's face, the "channel" changes (signified by the top right counter going up); the party and Tenna are now in the Cooking Show set, the party members dressed in their chef outfits. The channel changes again, and again, and again, going through the Rock Video set, the Monster Movie set, the cowboy game set, and finally returning to the TV Time set. This repeats over and over, faster and faster, until the channel counter reaches Ch66 and the screen abruptly "turns off", ending the encounter.
