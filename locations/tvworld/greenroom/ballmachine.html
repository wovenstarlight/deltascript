Gacha menu

The menu lists four fields, two in white towards the top and two in white towards the bottom. The below values are the initial defaults:

POINTs TO SPEND: 100 {%- comment %} removing whitespace {% endcomment -%}
HELD POINTs: <#>
GOLD CHANCE: 5%
SINCE LAST GOLD: 0

If the "GOLD CHANCE" percentage is at the maximum, it is printed in a brighter yellow as "GOLD CHANGE: 100%". The "SINCE LAST GOLD" value updates to show how many POINTs you've spent on all gambling attempts since obtaining your last gold prize. If all gold prizes have been obtained, the menu instead looks as follows:

POINTs TO SPEND: 100 {%- comment %} removing whitespace {% endcomment -%}
HELD POINTs: <#>
NO MORE GOLD

In both cases, there is text beneath the menu reading "[CONFIRM] CONFIRM" and "[CANCEL] CANCEL", with the appropriate buttons substituted in depending on the platform. On PC versions, these lines read "[Z] CONFIRM" and "[X] CANCEL" by default.

Upon inserting POINTs

Standard

When [CONFIRM] is selected, the scene cuts back to the Ball Machine room, showing golden coin-like POINTs being inserted into the gray slot on the front of the ball machine. The machine shakes briefly before a capsule is released from the bottom slot, floating up in front of the machine and coming apart to reveal the prize inside. See below for the narration/dialogue accompanying certain prizes.

After the prize-specific dialogue/narration plays, if your score is still at 100 POINTs or higher, then the following narration is shown:

If your score is instead below 100 POINTs after the prize-specific dialogue/narration, dialogue ends.

Upon backing out

When [CANCEL] is selected, the gacha menu closes, returning you to the Ball Machine room.

Gacha prizes

Unless specified otherwise, regular prizes can be won unlimited times, while gold prizes can only be won once.

Items

A small bag labelled with a question mark emerges from the capsule. If there is room in the inventory or storage, the generic item get message plays and the item is added to the inventory or storage, wherever there is room. Available items include:

  • Regular prizes:
    • Glowshard
    • TV Dinner
    • TV Slop
    • Deluxe Dinner
  • Gold prizes:
    • Revive Mint
    • Tension Max
    • Exec Buffet

If the inventory and storage are both full, then the following messages play instead:

(You have too many ITEMs to take <Item>.) (As a consolation, you received 100 POINTs.)

100 POINTs are added to your score, up to a maximum of 9999 POINTs. The counter for POINTs spent since your last gold prize does not increase.

Armor

A small bag labelled with a question mark emerges from the capsule. If there is room in the armor inventory, the generic armor get message plays and the armor is added to the inventory. Available armors include:

  • Regular prizes:

  • Gold prizes:
    • Tenna Tie
    • Blue Ribbon

If the armor inventory is full, then the following messages play instead:

(You have too many ARMORs to take <Armor>.) (As a consolation, you received 100 POINTs.)

100 POINTs are added to your score, up to a maximum of 9999 POINTs. The counter for POINTs spent since your last gold prize does not increase.

Inserting 1225 POINTs

Immediately upon pressing [CONFIRM], the oscillating stars in the background disappear, leaving behind a background of solid dark blue; the music also cuts out, and the arrows beside the "POINTs TO SPEND" value disappear. The screen is frozen for a few seconds.

Abruptly, the screen cuts back to the Ball Machine room, now tinted a dim blue. The user guide sign, Susie, and Ralsei are all missing. Kris is the only one in the room, still standing in front of the POINTs machine. Nothing in the room can be interacted with, and Kris cannot leave the room.

On the front of the Ball Machine, the red slot from which capsules emerge is missing. Instead, there is a black opening roughly the size of a door. Kris can walk up to this opening, at which point the screen will slowly fade to black.

The screen will then fade back in on a mysterious room with a dark blue checkered background over which a maze of red-carpeted walkways sprawl as a guitar version of Lost Girl plays. The room has no collision, allowing Kris to move off the walkways over thin air.

The room loops in all directions, and there is almost nothing within it. Towards the bottom right, however, is a small blue ball machine that appears completely empty. This is the only object in this entire room with collisions enabled, so that Kris cannot walk through it. Upon interacting with it from any direction, the following narration plays:

You used the machine. ... The capsule came out. Inside was something hard like lacquer. It was a small, dark, triangle. You tried to take it... But, it slipped through your hand And you couldn't find it anymore.

The music fades out quickly as soon as the previous line is completely printed, and the following narration plays slowly, unskippably, and soundlessly:

You couldn't find

your hand.

The screen slowly fades to black. Then it fades back in on Kris standing alone at the center of the Ball Machine room, now with the ball machine and POINTs machine absent, leaving it permanently and completely empty. There is no music playing. Kris can leave the room, at which point Susie and Ralsei are back in the party and the music resumes as normal, even upon reentering the Ball Machine room.

Upon reentering the room after this point, if the Sword Route has been completed and the Bibliox in "nowhere" has been spoken to, there is a glimmer on the ground where the Ball Machine used to be.

Trip Ticket

Available only after visiting the secret gacha room.

There is a glimmer on the ground where the Ball Machine once stood. It can be interacted with:

(You thought you saw something on the ground... but you must've already had it.)

With a rising chime, the Trip Ticket is added to the inventory. The glimmer on the ground disappears.
